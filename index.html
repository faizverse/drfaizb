Who I Am

About Me

Passionate about discovery. Devoted to the craft of teaching. Committed to making science matter.

I am an academic and researcher at the Faculty of Engineering and Built Environment, Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia (UKM). My work sits at the intersection of semiconductor physics, artificial intelligence, and engineering education — fields where I strive to bridge fundamental research with real-world impact.

With a University of Glasgow PhD and nearly two decades of academic experience, I have contributed to over 40 peer-reviewed publications, secured more than RM1 million in research funding, and built collaborations spanning industry, government, and international institutions. Beyond the laboratory and lecture hall, I am passionate about community engagement — from mentoring national robotics teams to writing for national newspapers on technology and society.

What I Investigate

Research Areas

Three interconnected domains where my lab pushes the frontier of knowledge and application.

Semiconductor Devices

Modelling, simulation, and characterisation of nano-scale MOSFETs and FinFETs, charge-trapping degradation, thin-film solar cells, and graphene-CNT hybrid nanostructures.

Artificial Intelligence

Deep learning for autonomous vehicle control, medical image segmentation, facial recognition attendance systems, and smart POS automation using computer vision.

Engineering Education

Innovative pedagogy including online distance learning, Arduino-based hands-on modules, and entrepreneurship integration into engineering curricula in collaboration with Stevens Institute of Technology, US.

A comprehensive national strategic study commissioned by the Academy of Sciences Malaysia to assess the role of science and technology in driving Malaysia's E&E sector growth. SAMS: Student Attendance Monitoring System

Project Leader Lead Developer

An early-generation mobile attendance system based on QR codes and smartphones, enabling lecturers to monitor class attendance in real time. Deployed across courses at UKM and recognised with a Bronze Medal at the 2015 UKM Teaching Innovation Carnival.

Registered copyright · Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia

Student Attendance Monitoring by Facial Recognition

Project Leader Lead Developer

An advanced evolution of SAMS leveraging computer vision and facial recognition to automate attendance recording with high accuracy. Eliminates manual input entirely, reducing time and preventing proxy attendance.

Registered copyright · Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia

Smart POS System with Automated Item Identification

Project Leader Lead Developer

An AI-powered Point-of-Sale system that uses computer vision to automatically identify retail items at self-checkout counters, reducing queues and labour costs. Developed as a proof-of-concept for the smart retail sector.
